Frites

What is frites?

Frites is the French word for French fries. In English-speaking countries, frites is used on menus to indicate that the fries are prepared in the Belgian or French style. But more often the word is simply used to make them sound fancier. Frites is typically used in the plural.

Ingredients | Steps | Tips

Ingredients

4 large russet potatoes, sliced
2 quarts vegetable or peanut oil
kosher salt
1/4 cup dijon mustard
2 tbsp honey
1/4 ketchup
1 teaspoon wasabi paste

Steps
  1. Peel potatoes and cut into 1/4-inch matchsticks. Submerge fries in cold water while you slice the others to prevent browning.
  2. Heat a large pot of oil to exactly 325°F. Use a deep fry thermometer!
  3. Dry fries well in batches and once dry, add slowly to oil. Fry the potatoes for 4 minutes then remove and let drain. They will be mostly cooked at this point, but not browned at all.
  4. When all the frites are fried, heat oil to 375°F.
  5. When oil is hot, cook the fries for a second time in the hotter oil for 3-4 minutes. They should turn perfectly crispy and brown.
  6. Remove fries from oil and let drain on a few paper towels. Season fries immediately with salt while hot.
  7. Serve fries with wasabi ketchup and/or honey mustard.
5 tips for making perfect frites
  1. First of all, you have to choose a good potato. Remember to peel and cut your potatoes at the last moment, to keep them fresh.
    Once the fries are cut, dry them before frying.
  2. For frying, use unrefined beef fat. This is also called "beef white" and do not forget to change the fat every 8 to 10 times. If you only make chips rarely, you will need to replace the oil after a while.
  3. Very important, then you need to cook french fries twice. First beat the French fries for 6 minutes at 130-140 ° C, then let stand and cook your fries a second time for 1.5 to 3 minutes according to 165 ° C-170 ° C, depending on the size of french fries.
  4. The higher the cooking temperature, the faster the grease or oil will age.
  5. Do not leave chips in the frying.
